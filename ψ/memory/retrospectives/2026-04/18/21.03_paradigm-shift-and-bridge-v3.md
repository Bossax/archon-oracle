## Honest Feedback
**Friction Points:**
1. **Security Oversight**: My initial "Shared Volume" design for mandates was a major security breach of Oracle isolation. I must remain more vigilant about brain sovereignty.
2. **Deterministic Argument Parsing**: The initial regex-based identity extraction in `init-bridge.ts` was brittle. Moving to a CLI-argument-driven model with AI pre-summarization is much more reliable but requires more discipline from the agent.
3. **Registry Schema Migration**: The manual migration of `registry.json` was prone to error. In the future, the `oracle-lab` should have a built-in migration utility for schema changes.

## Lessons Learned
- **The "Dumb Zone" is Real**: AI performance degrades significantly when instruction files exceed 60 lines. Modularization is not just "clean code"; it is a cognitive necessity for agents.
- **Neutral Sourcing**: Shared mandates must be agent-agnostic. Specificity (Name, Tone) must be injected during deployment, not hardcoded in the source.
- **Actors Over Tools**: Tracking what an agent *is capable of* provides better systemic awareness than tracking where a tool is installed.
